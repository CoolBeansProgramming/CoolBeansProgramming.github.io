About Me

My Background

I have always loved math and science. In college, I was originally an astrophysics major. I switched universities after my freshman year and studied physics and astronomy. Some time later, I decided to pursue mathematics instead and minored in actuarial science and physics. I took many, many math and statistics courses, took a few computer science courses, and an introductory statistics course. It was in this course that I learned how much I enjoyed learning about and using statistics. I took a lot more courses in the field and obtained my masters degree in applied statistics.

My Current Role

As an analyst in the banking industry, I use data to deliver actionable insights to better help customers and executives. I am excited to be at the forefront analyzing large amounts of data that will have real world results.


In my current role, I primarily use Microsoft Excel and Tableau but have also learned other skills through self-teaching and academic coursework including Microsoft Power BI, R, SAS, Google Sheets, and am familiar with Python.

What I'm Doing Now

I am currently learning new data analysis tools and am developing programming skills. As I learn, I will apply my skills to finance related data. I plan to use all skills I have learned so far to analyze and present this data and my results. I am currently learning Python (NumPy, Pandas) which will give me a good foundation as I move on to studying machine learning concepts.
